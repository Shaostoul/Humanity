HumanityOS

Active January 2026 to Present

A decentralized, self-hostable, open-source operating system designed to end poverty in pursuit of a peaceful, harmoniously united humanity. One binary that works as both a full desktop application and a headless server. Every device is a node. Every node empowers its community.

Communication

Peer-to-peer end-to-end encrypted messaging. No middleman reads your data. Servers are optional relays, not requirements.

Organization

Task management, project coordination, marketplace with P2P trading. Tools for communities to self-organize effectively.

Education

A 3D simulation teaching real survival skills: gardening, construction, crafting, resource management. Skills that transfer to real life.

Decentralization

No home servers. No accounts controlled by corporations. Your identity is a cryptographic key. Your data stays yours.

If we must use their systems to survive, we are their slaves. The only path to freedom is building alternative systems that function so profoundly differently that their control becomes meaningless.

Shaostoul, April 2026

Project Universe

Origin January 2019 to January 2026

The original vision: an immersive educational game where players develop real-world self-sufficiency skills through engaging gameplay in a lifelike environment. More than just a game. A platform to end poverty, unite humanity, and prepare for interplanetary civilization.

Ending poverty is the key to uniting humanity. If we can teach every person on Earth the skills to sustain themselves and their communities, scarcity becomes a choice rather than a circumstance.

Shaostoul, January 2019

Timeline

December 24, 2017
The Decision
After a week in the ICU fighting sepsis, Michael "Shaostoul" Boisson returned home on Christmas Eve and dedicated his life to humanity's betterment. Seven years of chronic illness had stripped away everything except one question: what would make this life worth living? The answer was to pursue the wildest possible dream: end poverty and unite humanity.
March 2018
Emerald City Comic Con
Gave a standing-room-only presentation on the benefits of video game modding for individuals, communities, and developers. People were turned away at the door for fire safety. This confirmed the appetite for games that teach real skills.
January 29, 2019
Project Universe Launches
Officially invited humanity to voluntarily join in researching and building solutions to poverty. Open source from day one. No paywalls. Everything free to use, modify, and enhance. Development began with CryEngine, later moved to Unity.
May 2023
First Playable Build
Project Universe v1.0.0 released. Basic gameplay loop with resource management, building, and crafting. The vision was proven possible. But the architecture needed to be more ambitious.
January 2026
Becomes HumanityOS
The project evolved beyond a game into a full civilization operating system. Renamed to HumanityOS to reflect the expanded scope: decentralized communication, P2P identity, marketplace, governance tools, and an educational simulation engine. Same mission, broader reach. Rebuilt from scratch in Rust for performance, security, and portability.

Original Vision

Real-World Parity

Every real-world skill and system has a faithful in-game counterpart. Farming, welding, electronics, construction, medicine, cooking, navigation.

Fantasy as Teaching

Realistic simulation alone isn't enough. Sci-fi scenarios provide the excitement that makes players want to learn real skills.

Cooperative Design

Built around cooperation, not competition. Players help each other, share knowledge, and build together.

Open Source

Code, design documents, governance. All open and transparent. Public domain. No corporation controls the future of this platform.

Modding Career

1994 to 2019

25 years of game modding formed the foundation for everything. Learning by doing, teaching by sharing, building community through shared creativity. From modifying Amiga games at age 6 to creating WarCraft 3 custom games, Dwarf Fortress guides, Space Engineers mods, and PBR texture tutorials. Every mod taught a real skill. Every tutorial built community trust.

1994
First Mods
Age 6. Modified games on an Amiga 4000: Frontier Elite 2, Cannon Fodder, Master of Magic. Learned that games are just data you can change.
2003
Custom Games
Created custom games for WarCraft 3: The Frozen Throne. First experience building complete gameplay systems from scratch.
2006
Teaching Others
Began creating modding tutorials for Dwarf Fortress. Discovered that teaching others to mod was more fulfilling than modding alone.
2014
Space Engineers & PBR
Mastered Physically Based Rendering through Space Engineers modding. Created detailed tutorials teaching others the same skills. This directly influenced HumanityOS's data-driven, moddable architecture.
