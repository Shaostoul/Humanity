Frequently Asked Questions

What are funds used for?
Full-time development of HumanityOS, server and infrastructure costs, and building open-source tools that remain free for everyone. Every dollar is accounted for and spent on making this platform better for all of humanity.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Sponsor-A-Can is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit, so donations go through a registered charity. Whether your gift is tax-deductible depends on your country and situation, so check the footer at sponsor-a-can.org or ask a tax advisor. Donations are not earmarked to HumanityOS specifically; they support Sponsor-A-Can, the nonprofit that employs HumanityOS's maintainer. Crypto sent to the wallet addresses below is a direct, non-custodial gift to the maintainer and is not processed by the nonprofit.
Why crypto?
Zero fees, no intermediaries, and full transparency. Crypto aligns with HumanityOS's values of sovereignty and self-custody. Blockchain transactions are publicly verifiable, so anyone can audit where funds go. Solana uses Ed25519 signatures, the same cryptography HumanityOS uses for identity.
Is Coinbase or an exchange OK?
It works, but self-custody is better. When you send from an exchange, the exchange controls the keys and can freeze your account. A self-custody wallet (like Phantom for Solana or Sparrow for Bitcoin) means you control your funds directly. Either way, your donation reaches us the same.
