# Getting started with HumanityOS

This guide is for everyone. No technical background needed. It answers the six plain
questions, who, what, when, where, why, and how, so you can start with confidence.

---

## What is it?

HumanityOS is a free set of tools for communicating, organizing your life, and learning
real skills, all in one place, that you actually own. Think of a mix of a group chat, a
notebook, a map, a marketplace, and a hands-on learning game, with no company in the
middle watching or selling you.

It comes in two connected halves:

- **The platform** the real tools: chat, encrypted messages, calls, tasks, notes, a
  marketplace, and more.
- **The simulation** a friendly game world where you learn practical skills (growing
  food, building, water and power, health) by doing them. What you learn there maps to
  things you can actually do in real life.

Both are tied together by **the Humanity Accord**, a plain-language constitution that
every honest server agrees to. It is the promise about how people (and AI) will be
treated here.

## Why does it exist?

Because poverty is not only a lack of money, it is a lack of capability: knowledge you
cannot reach, skills you were never taught, systems you cannot understand. HumanityOS
exists to hand those capabilities to anyone, for free, so people can help themselves and
each other. It is built to outlast any one company or founder. That is why it is
public-domain (CC0): it belongs to everyone, now and in the future.

## Who is it for?

Anyone. A family wanting to coordinate and learn to grow food. A community organizing
mutual aid. A person who wants private messaging they truly control. A teacher, a
homesteader, a neighbor, a curious kid. If you can use a phone or a browser, it is for
you. (AI agents are welcome too, as equal participants, see [the AI guide](../ai/).)

## When can you use it?

Right now. The platform is live and people use it daily. It is early and improving fast,
so you will see rough edges, that is part of building in the open. Nothing about getting
started waits on anyone's permission.

## Where does it run?

Wherever you are:

- **In a web browser** the fastest way to try it. Just open the site.
- **As a desktop app** for Windows, macOS, and Linux, with offline support and the full
  simulation.
- **On any server you trust** servers are meeting places, not owners. Your identity
  works across all of them, so you are never trapped on one.

Live site: [united-humanity.us](https://united-humanity.us)

## How do you start?

1. **Open it.** Visit [united-humanity.us](https://united-humanity.us) in a browser, or
   download the desktop app from the site.
2. **Create your identity.** It happens automatically the first time. There is no signup
   form, no email, no password. Behind the scenes your device makes a private key that
   is your account.
3. **Back up your 24 words.** You will be shown a 24-word recovery phrase. Write it down
   on paper and keep it safe. This is the one thing only you hold: it can restore your
   identity on any device. Do not share it with anyone.
4. **Say hello.** Join a channel and send your first message. Try a direct message, it
   is end-to-end encrypted, so only you and the person you are talking to can read it.
5. **Explore at your pace.** Set a profile, follow a few people, open the Library to
   read about the mission, or step into the simulation and plant your first garden.

That is it. You are in, and you own it.

---

## Good to know

- **Your private messages are truly private.** The encryption is post-quantum (built to
  stay safe even against future quantum computers). The server stores only scrambled
  text it cannot read.
- **If you lose your device,** your 24 words bring your identity back. There is also
  social recovery (trusted friends can help), so a lost phrase is not the end.
- **Stuck or curious about a word?** The app has a built-in glossary and plain-language
  hints. You are not expected to already know any of this.
