# 2026-07-17..19 - The planet-quality marathon (v0.874 - v0.886)

One continuous session driving Earth from "tech demo globe" to "a place
you can fly to, land on, and live above." Thirteen releases, each
operator-field-report driven; the operator went AFK at the end and loop
mode shipped the final three solo.

## Shipped, in order

- **v0.874 - live weather.** NASA GIBS MODIS cloud fraction fetched in
  the background, decoded via the official palette to an RG8
  placement mask the cloud shader blends (validity channel falls back
  to procedural). Verified against the same-day satellite reference.
- **v0.875 - the 1 m terrain ladder.** PatchId.path u32 -> u64, tile
  depth cap 16 -> 20 (~0.42 m triangles near the ground), fine-noise
  octaves extended 125 m -> 1 m (Nyquist gates 14..20).
- **v0.876/877 - the ocean split.** True bathymetric seafloor under a
  translucent Gerstner-displaced water shell (material type 16); CPU
  wave twin locked to the shader by a constants guard test; the player
  floats on sea + wave height. v0.877 moved the shell to the
  transparent pass.
- **v0.878 - sun-frame unification.** One relation ties clock, spin,
  and sun: spin = sun_azimuth + (hour-12) * TAU/24. Noon means the sun
  is over the prime meridian; lighting became stageable and the HUD
  clock stopped lying about daylight.
- **v0.879 - field-report fixes.** ONE cached spin per frame (the six
  v0.878 call sites straddled the TimeSystem tick = the constant
  flicker + swimming shores), MODIS swath-slit inpainting, water-shell
  skirts removed + displacement distance-fade, FTL approach governor
  (step <= half the distance to the surface).
- **v0.880 - unified flight.** The 10-100 km band honors the full
  wheel gear under the governor (the stuck-at-exactly-100.0km
  oscillation), radial-wish dead zone (W no longer floats you off the
  ground), ONE hotbar, towering cumulus (coverage raises the band
  top), swath-boundary blur.
- **v0.881 - the home leaves the player.** The homestead became a real
  station on a 400 km LEO orbit (period ~92.6 real min, phase =
  wall-clock UTC). Aboard, the player frame rides the orbit so every
  home system works unchanged; away, one pass-level translation places
  the whole scene at the station. Spawn puts you aboard: the ISS view.
  Killed the deck-photobombs-every-screenshot problem for good.
- **v0.882 - steady terrain.** LOD split/merge hysteresis (residency
  is the memory), water lifted 1.2 m off the beach line, flight band
  flies where you look.
- **v0.883 - the stationary fixed point.** Budget check BEFORE build
  requests: a saturated tree stops commissioning builds it cannot
  draw, so a parked camera converges instead of churning through the
  build->evict->rebuild wave. v0.883.2 set TRUE-SCALE relief
  (surface_relief 0.011 -> 0.003123): Fuji got its real proportions
  back and the cliff-terrace look died with the 3.5x exaggeration.
- **v0.884 - smooth terrain normals.** Per-vertex averaged normals;
  the flat-shaded 0.3 m quantization ledges (the Minecraft steps)
  melt into sweeping slopes.
- **v0.885 (loop) - target markers v1.** The operator's design: ring +
  label + distance on tracked world targets (v1 = the home station),
  Cosmos page "Stations" section with the Track toggle; tile-aware
  altitude parking (no more spawning inside Fuji).
- **v0.886 (loop) - the sun's corona.** Shader type 17 radial glow on
  a 3x shell; the halo material had existed since the beginning but
  was never drawn - the white blob was the bare core. track_station
  persisted in AppConfig.

## Lessons that went into guards

- naga silently accepts an @vertex/@fragment attribute orphaned onto a
  const by an insertion between attribute and fn; the module validates
  with ZERO entry points and every pipeline dies at first boot. The
  shader_loader test now pins entry points by name.
- Two clock reads inside one frame are two different clocks: any
  spin/time value consumed by both physics and render must be computed
  once per frame (v0.872's lesson, relearned as v0.879's fix).
- A stateless LOD selection is only stable if its INPUTS are stable:
  build requests that outrun the draw budget mutate the cache forever.
  Budget first, then requests.
- Test bands must model production steady state: a fat +-200 m radial
  band made every patch within 200 m tie at the screen-error distance
  floor and saturate the leaf budget one level shy of the cap.
