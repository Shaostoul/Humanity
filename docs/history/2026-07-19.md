# 2026-07-19 - The overnight loop (v0.889 - v0.896)

The operator went to bed with a five-item list and an instruction: loop all
night, work maximally, no approval checkpoints, spend the remaining Fable
budget before the tier shuts off. Eight feature releases shipped by morning,
each verified on a new probe rig before release.

## The bedtime list (all shipped)

- **Terrain prefetch** (v0.889.0): leaves past 0.55x of the split threshold
  request their unbuilt children early (capped 12/selection, skipped at
  budget saturation), so camera motion crosses LOD thresholds into
  already-resident meshes. This was the operator's "cache/preload the
  detail" idea against movement flicker, almost verbatim.
- **See-through-Earth fix** (v0.889.0): LOD_CLEARANCE_M 2.5 added to the
  shared floor of the surface clamp AND rest_radius - the drawn coarse patch
  can bulge above the full-detail clamp model on ridges, putting the eye
  under the triangles.
- **F6 location bookmarks** (v0.890.0): saves the exact camera pose in the
  planet's unrotated frame to debug/bookmarks.json (spin-independent);
  restore via camera_request {"bookmark":"bm-N"|"last"}. Screenshot
  teleports now land exactly where the operator stood and looked.
- **Q/E flight roll** (v0.890.0): view-only bank around the look axis
  (~80 deg/s) in fly mode; eases level on foot; Q keeps shoulder-swap on
  foot, E stays interact.
- **Flight transition smoothing** (v0.890.0): the 100-1000 km blend band
  rides a smoothstep fraction of the planet's spin, killing the ~465 m/s
  one-frame tangential yank at the co-rotate boundary in both directions.

## The self-directed run

- **4x frame rate** (v0.891.0): the 15-FPS-at-3072-patches ceiling was CPU
  submission, not GPU: one queue.write_buffer per object per pass (3000+
  calls/frame) plus full material rebinds per object. One batched staging
  upload per pass + bound-material elision. Measured on the identical
  Everest scene, 2048 draws saturated: 19.8 -> 78.6 FPS (50.4 -> 12.0 ms).
- **Budget ceiling 6144 / default 3072 / MAX_OBJECTS 8192** (v0.892.0).
- **7 cloud families** (v0.893.0): + altocumulus, cumulonimbus,
  nimbostratus interleaved so the original four keep their type anchors;
  regime tests extended. FXC lesson: naga's HLSL backend cannot pass
  array<f32,N> across function boundaries (X3017 crashed pipeline
  creation) - the blend is one scalar-accumulator loop now.
- **Persistent trees** (v0.894.0): trees now spawn at every depth past 15
  with a fixed-point accept keeping per-AREA density constant - previously
  they vanished when a patch refined past depth 17, so forests existed only
  at ridge distance. Density baseline x4.
- **God rays** (v0.895.0): one additive full-screen pass marching the DEPTH
  buffer toward the sun between the celestial and scene passes - terrain
  silhouettes carve real shafts; sky taps pass light. Verified with the new
  camera_request {"aim":"sun"} rig after an hour of blind time-staging
  proved sun-in-frame shots are pure luck (the probe world's clock runs
  ~77x, so staged times expire in real seconds).
- **Biome gate + lit vegetation + ocean-park backstops** (v0.896.0):
  vegetation only where the surface imagery is green-dominant (kills Sahara
  trees, glacier trees); cards shade like the ground (their plane normal
  gave N.L ~ 0 at noon = black slabs); ocean parking is radius-gated in
  both the camera_request and walk-clamp paths (the mask misses fjords and
  can still be loading when scripted requests fire - an Iceland park kept
  diving under the water shell).

## The day sprint (operator awake; v0.897 - v0.900)

Morning field reports drove a debugging arc that ended the terrain
flicker for real and gave the world its first true shadows:

- **v0.897**: vegetation moved to a planet-fixed cell grid (same plants at
  every LOD depth - splits stopped reshuffling the forest); god rays
  switched to a screen blend (bright cloud decks can no longer blow out)
  plus live-overcast dimming; the distant-planet sliders were labeled so
  nobody hunts them for ground detail again.
- **v0.898**: the flicker autopsy. Two root causes, both proven with the
  probe's ChunkDiag telemetry: the 256 MB patch cache (sized for 640-leaf
  budgets years of releases ago) forced permanent build->evict->rebuild
  waves at 6144-leaf budgets, and the prefetch had hollowed out the
  residency-keyed split hysteresis into a dense oscillation zone. Fixes:
  1.5 GB cache + never-evict-recent guard, hysteresis re-keyed on the
  last-frame DRAWN set, and a committed-split budget tier so the budget
  wall cannot wander. Proof: five consecutive seconds of byte-identical
  selections parked at 6144. Also: cloud ground shadows + land detail
  octaves down to 8 m.
- **v0.899**: the sun shadow map. 4096^2 near-field ortho depth from the
  sun, texel-snapped, 3x3 PCF, depth-only vs_main variant so ocean waves
  cast; every lit fragment receives (interior included). Two wgpu panics
  taught: bind the lights storage buffer in any camera-layout group, and
  never sample the texture a pass writes (dummy-depth group-3 variant).
- **v0.900**: shadow-caster distance cull (the map only covers ~1.5 km;
  don't rasterize the whole planet into it).

Fable signs off with the planet flicker-free, shadowed, vegetated, and
weather-lit. The remaining environmental wants are listed in PRIORITIES.
